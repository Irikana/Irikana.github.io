Introduction

What is Shepherd's Library?

Shepherd's Library (also known as Shpherd's Library) is a fictional concept I created — it is where I store my imagination and creations. Therefore, it can be broadly understood as anything capable of fulfilling this purpose: it could be a book, a notebook, or even a folder.

History

Origin of the Concept

Shepherd's Library was first created on December 31, 2022, in my digital artwork titled "Shpherd's Library." Although the artwork contained some typos (such as writing "Shpherd" instead of "Shepherd"), notably, following my naming conventions, "Shepherd" felt too common; to preserve the library's uniqueness, "Shpherd" also serves as an alternative proper noun to add distinctiveness and avoid confusion with any other library. This artwork is still recognized as the first creation of Shepherd's Library. However, this merely represents the complete motivation — such as when I truly wanted to start preserving my thoughts in one place. My actual first use of the term "Shepherd's Library" may have been even earlier.

To my knowledge, the earliest recorded use of "Shepherd's Library" (牧羊人图书馆) dates to November 4, 2022, first appearing in my artwork "Shepherd's Library" during my sophomore year of high school (though the artwork was not yet complete at that time). Although much content inspired by the concept itself was incorporated, I am certain that the intended meaning was indeed this.

Sources of Inspiration

The following content includes quoted text from external encyclopedias (Quark Encyclopedia / Baidu Baike) and the author's personal summary, provided for background reference only.

From 2017 to 2021, I was deeply drawn to SCP Foundation-related content. If you don't know what SCP Foundation is, you can quickly search encyclopedia entries via any browser's search engine, or read my summary below:

SCP Foundation (Quark Encyclopedia)

SCP Foundation is an online collaborative fiction project with a theme of weird science fiction, originating from a horror story about numbered SCP-173 on the American 4chan forum in 2007. Through formats simulating research reports and archival records, the project describes various supernatural phenomena, anomalous entities, and objects (collectively called "SCPs"), and constructs a fictional organization — the SCP Foundation — responsible for containing, controlling, and researching these anomalies to maintain normalcy in human society. The Foundation operates independently of national governments, with its core principle being "Secure, Contain, Protect." As the project expanded, SCP has spawned multilingual divisions, comics, games, videos, and other cross-media works. In 2021, due to some content being deemed harmful to minors, the "SCP series" was classified as "childhood harmful material" and removed across China.

SCP Foundation Universe (Baidu Baike)

The SCP universe is a fictional universe system constructed by the online weird fiction series "SCP Foundation," containing core elements including anomalous containment objects, peer organizations, and supernatural concepts. Its world-building consists of multiple independently-created branch systems interwoven together. As the primary institution, the Foundation coexists with rival or cooperative organizations such as the Global Occult Coalition and the Church of the Broken God, allowing contradictory settings across different works.

This universe encompasses supernatural concepts such as thaumaturgy, memetics, and narrative layers, extending into branch systems like the AAPA interstellar civilization framework and the Death-Ending Event. Peer organizations like the Chaos Insurgency oppose the Foundation through anomalous item trading, while the Factory possesses mass-production capabilities for anomalous items, and the Serpent's Hand opposes the containment philosophy itself.

The Multiverse Setting Center covers parallel universe frameworks ranging from future-science-fiction-oriented Third Law, to "Unfounded" series without the Foundation, to the absurdist-toned Happy Foundation. Each branch system forms a loosely connected composite narrative network through author collaboration.

SCP Foundation (My Summary)

SCP Foundation is the name of an online fictional collaborative literature website, generally referring to all content on that site, covering themes of weird fiction, science fiction, and alternate history. SCP Foundation has established nearly a unique worldview and timeline independent from modern society, allowing for the existence of supernatural phenomena. It tells the story of a dark organization that, deep within an unfathomable abyss, Secures and Contains all supernatural entities while secretly Protecting humanity. The organization is governed by the O5 Council.

In essence, or from a real-world perspective, SCP Foundation consists of a group of active writers on the internet who collaborate on writing and even illustration. These writers unleash their boundless imagination here, jointly constructing this virtual universe. They even consider each other's works during their creative process, thereby creating interconnections among themselves, which greatly increases the perceived authenticity of the Foundation's content.

The Wanderers' Library (Quark Encyclopedia)

The Wanderers' Library is a fictional location within the online weird fiction series "SCP Foundation," and also serves as a sister website to the SCP Foundation creative platform. The Library exists in a dimension beyond conventional space-time, housing nearly all knowledge texts that have been written or remain unwritten across every universe and reality, encompassing books, poetry, audio, video, and many other forms. Its core theme is "the sense of wonder and mystery" — revealing strange existences and phenomena hidden at the edges of reality through fragmented narrative, evoking awe and curiosity toward the unknown world. Since the late 19th century, multiple supernatural organizations, especially the Serpent's Hand, have established informal connections with the Library, using it as a venue for knowledge sharing and gatherings. In 1967, the Serpent's Hand rediscovered the secret path to the Library, marking its renewed activity within contemporary supernatural communities.

The Wanderers' Library (Baidu Baike)

The Wanderers' Library is a location within the online weird fiction series "SCP Foundation," and simultaneously a sister website to the SCP Foundation creative platform.

The Wanderers' Library is the central repository of all knowledge spanning every universe and dimension. Its endless halls house every book from the past or future (some of which may never even be written). Scholars from unknown worlds lurk through the corridors, rummaging through wastebaskets for forbidden knowledge. Reading even just one shelf of the Library would take multiple lifetimes.

BioShock Infinite (Baidu Baike)

BioShock Infinite is a first-person shooter developed by Irrational Games and published by 2K Games, released on March 26, 2013.

The game's setting is an alternate early 20th century, a golden age filled with massive airships and hot air balloon aircraft. Players embark on a series of adventures in the sky-city of "Columbia."
